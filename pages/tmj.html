Temporomandibular Joint Disorder

What is TMJ?

TMJ is the jaw joint that helps you open and close your mouth. Most TMJ issues are not serious and can improve with simple care.

TMJ Symptoms and Problems

Common signs and triggers of temporomandibular joint disorder

Common Symptoms

TMJ problems can cause jaw pain, clicking sounds, difficulty opening mouth, or headaches that affect daily activities.

Pain Triggers

Pain may increase with chewing hard foods, stress, or jaw clenching - especially during times of tension or anxiety.

Manageable Condition

Most TMJ issues are not serious and can improve with proper care and lifestyle adjustments under dental guidance.

Management – Easy Points

Simple strategies to manage and relieve TMJ discomfort

Soft Diet

Avoid hard or chewy foods to reduce strain on the jaw joint during healing.

Warm Compress

Apply warm compress on the jaw to relax tense muscles and improve circulation.

Jaw Exercises

Follow exercises given by dentist/physiotherapist to improve jaw movement and flexibility.

Avoid Harmful Habits

Stop habits like nail biting, teeth grinding, or excessive gum chewing that strain the jaw.

Stress Control

Manage stress through relaxation techniques as stress often causes jaw clenching.

Pain Management

Use simple painkillers as advised by the dentist for temporary relief of discomfort.

Advanced Care

Severe or long-lasting cases may need splints/night guards or specialist care for proper treatment.

Most Cases are Manageable

The majority of TMJ disorders respond well to conservative treatment methods. With proper care, dietary adjustments, and stress management, most people experience significant improvement in symptoms and return to normal jaw function.

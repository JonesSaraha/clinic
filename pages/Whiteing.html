Teeth Whitening Treatment

Bleaching in dentistry is a cosmetic procedure where special whitening agents (usually hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide) are applied to the teeth to lighten their color and remove stains.

Bleaching Procedure for Teeth

Step-by-step guide to teeth whitening treatment

Lightens Tooth Colour

Bleaching uses safe whitening gels to make your teeth several shades lighter, removing years of stains and discoloration.

Done at Clinic or at Home

The dentist can whiten your teeth in one visit, or give you custom trays to use bleaching gel at home for gradual whitening.

Quick and Comfortable

The procedure is usually painless, taking about 30–60 minutes in the clinic with immediate visible results.

May Cause Temporary Sensitivity

Some people may feel mild sensitivity for a day or two, which goes away on its own without any treatment.

Needs Good After-Care

Avoid tea, coffee, smoking, or coloured foods for 24–48 hours to keep the whitening results stable and long-lasting.

Important After-Care Note
To maintain your bright smile, avoid staining substances like tea, coffee, red wine, and tobacco for at least 48 hours after treatment. Follow your dentist's specific after-care instructions for optimal results.

ROOT CANAL TREATMENT

A root canal is a treatment to save a badly infected or painful tooth, relieving pain and preserving your natural smile.

About Root Canal Treatment

Root canal treatment (endodontic therapy) is a dental procedure used to treat infection at the center of a tooth (the pulp). It's performed when the pulp becomes infected or inflamed due to deep decay, repeated dental procedures, cracks, or trauma.

The Root Canal Procedure

1
Diagnosis and Preparation

When the pulp gets infected due to deep cavity, crack, or injury, it causes severe pain.

The dentist examines the tooth, takes X-rays, and administers local anesthesia to numb the area.

2
Removing Infected Pulp

In root canal treatment, the dentist removes the infected pulp from inside the tooth.

A small opening is made in the crown of the tooth to access the pulp chamber.

3
Cleaning and Shaping

The inside of the tooth is then cleaned, shaped, and disinfected.

Special instruments are used to remove bacteria and debris from the root canals.

4
Filling the Canals

After cleaning, the space is filled with a safe, sealing material (gutta-percha) to prevent infection.

The canals are sealed completely to prevent future contamination.

5
Restoration

Finally, the tooth is restored with a filling and often a crown (cap) to strengthen it.

The crown protects the treated tooth and restores its full function.

Key Benefits

Relieves severe tooth pain and discomfort
Prevents spread of infection to adjacent teeth
Preserves your natural tooth and smile
Restores normal chewing and biting function

Root canal treatment has a high success rate and can save a tooth that might otherwise need to be extracted.

Consult with your dentist to determine if root canal treatment is right for you.
